INVESTMENT TERMINOLOGY
  • • BETA - Beta is the numeric value that is used to measure the volatility of a stock againt changes occuring in the stock market
  • • BLUE CHIP COMPANY - A company that is worth billions of dollars, pays dividens, and has a long history of being reliable. These are some of the most safe investments to make
  • • BOND - A debt investment where investors loan funds to a corporation or government for a particular period (think of it as an IOU). An investor makes money off of the interest that is agreed on in the bond contract
  • • CAPITAL GAINS - Taxable event that happens when a stock or option is sold for a profit. Almost any asset owned by a person is a capital asset.
  • • DAY TRADING - Trading multiple stocks in a single day; requires a special account with atleast $25,000 of buying power
  • • DIVERGENCE - Trading concept that is created when a stock's price diverges from a momentum oscillator, indicating a reveral
  • • IPO - When a company sells a fixed # of stocks in order to raise money. The money is then used for future growth
  • • LAGGING INDICATORS - Technicals that trail economic performance (Moving Average, MA Convergence Divergence). Much more accurate than a leading indicator
  • • LEADING INDICATORS - Measurable performances that shifts ahead of the economic cycle before it happens. More risky than a lagging indicator
  • • LIMIT ORDER - The max an investor is willing to pay for a stock. Caution: If price moves fast it may never fill
  • • MARKET CAP - Measures a companies size. The total value of a companies shares owned by stockholders. Equal to the market price per share multiplied by the number of shares outstanding
  • • MARKET HOURS - The time the stock market is open. 9:30AM - 4PM EST Monday-Friday. The stock market is never open during national holidays.
  • • MERGER - Deals that unite 2 seperate companies into 1 single company. When mergers occur, it commonly drives the stock prices of both companies up
  • • MOMENTUM OSCILLATOR - Measures the amount a securities price has changed over a given time. It can be used to predict chart movement
  • • MOVING AVERAGES - Relative trends to price. Calculated by taking the sum of (X)-days and dividing by X
  • • PENNY STOCK - Any stock under $5. While they cost very little, they are often the most risky types of stocks as they are highly volatile
  • • PRICE TARGET - The projected price of a financial instrument as provided by an analyst.
  • • PUMP & DUMP - An investment scheme where untrue statements are used to influence investors. This creates a hype for the stock, and causes the price to drive up. The people running the scheme let this happen until they want to exit the trades, at which the price plummets, and screws most investors.
  • • RESISTANCE LEVEL - The price which a stock doesn't go higher than. This is a super reassuring way to guess where to exit a position.
  • • REVERSAL - A direction change of wherever a stock is currently moving. If the stock is moving up, a reversal means it starts moving down. If the stock is moving down, a reversal means it starts moving up
  • • ROI - Return on investment. How much revenue was generated from an investment. An annual ROI of approximately 7% or greater is considered a good ROI
  • • SHARE BUYBACK - Company that has IPO'd and is now buying their own shares back. This results in everyones share value increasing
  • • SHARES OUTSTANDING - The total amount of shares held by all investors
  • • SPLIT - Stock splits will change the price of a stock. When a company splits, it makes it easier for potential investors to buy shares because the price will drop.
  • • STOP ORDER - Can be used for getting in or out of a stock; when a stocks price moves past the limit it fulfills that order
  • • SUPPORT LEVEL - The price of a stock where demand is strong enough to not break the line. This is one of the best ways to base investments off of with technical analysis
  • • SWING TRADING - Holding stocks overnight & more than a day but still short term (a couple days to weeks)
  • • VOLATILITY - A measure of a stock's stability. If a stock is less volatile, it means price isn't as likely to shoot up or down as much but is more safe & assuring to generate profits
  • • VOLUME - Vertical lines at the bottom of a stock screener chart. Represent the # of trades made during a period of time
