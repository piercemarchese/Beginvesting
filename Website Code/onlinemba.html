THE BASICS OF AN MBA

GENERAL IDEAS AND TERMS

  • • Observe products & services around you
    1. – What, if any, is the competition for those products?
    2. – There are 2 ways to beat your competition: price and quality. Analyze the competitions weak points and target that
  • • Find a problem in your life and see how you can solve the problem. Think of what markets you know and can improve?
  • • Who is your customer?
    1. – Demographics: Age, gender, income, education level
    2. – Psychographics: What is your customer like? What do they wear? How do they think?
    3. – What problem are you solving: You need to be able to describe this in a humanly-sounding sentence
  • • Unique Value Propsition: Do you understand the painpoint you are solving?
    1. – Painpoint: The aspect of your product that makes customers want to buy it
  • • Talk to your customers
    1. – Get feedback to improve your product
    2. – Would your customers actually pay for this? It's important to be honest with yourself before launching the product
  • •Test your product and be willing to pivot. This means that you change the direction of a business in response to what the market wants
  • •Recognize that as an entrepreneur, you will do EVERYTHING when starting a business



THE 2 WAYS TO RUN A COMAPNY

  • • Bootstrap:
    1. – You only have your own money to use
    2. – You have full ownershp and control of profits BUT have to make money to get money
    3. – Slower to grow but more profitable in the long run
  • • Venture Capatilism
    1. – Someone gives you money to fund and put into your company
    2. – In exchange for this, they take on percentage of your company; can be seen as a form of collateral
    3. – Faster to grow but less profitable in the long run
  • • How to Pick?
    1. – This depends on the type of lifestyle you want to have
    2. – If you want to grow a company at your own rate, you should choose bootstrapping
    3. – If you need funding and accept to have a "boss" who will demand quick returns and growth, you should choose venture capatilism
