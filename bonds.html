HOW BONDS WORK AND HOW TO MAKE MONEY OFF THEM



Bonds represent a loan make by an investor to a borrower. In simple terms, it is an IOU between 2 parties. Bonds are used by companies, states, and sovereign governments to finance projects. Bonds are also referred to as FIXED INCOME SECURITIES because they require payments in the form of interest payments; if interest rates on new bonds go up, the value of old bonds go down because they aren't wanted as much since with a higher interest rate, the creditors make more money. The opposite is also true: If new bonds have lower interest rates than old ones, prices of old bonds increases and new bond prices lower because the creditor can't make as much off the interest payments.



CREDITOR VS. DEBTOR
  • CREDITOR - A person or company to whom money is owed. AKA a lender
  • DEBTOR - A person or company that owes money. AKA a borrower
  • EXAMPLE: If you take out a mortgage to finance a home, you're the debtor and the mortgage company is the creditor

ASPECTS OF A BOND
  • COUPON - The amount of interest on the bond; how much the creditor makes per interest payment
  • COUPON DATES - Days which the issuer must make interest payements
  • COUPON RATE - The annual interest rate established when the bond is issued
  • FACE VALUE - The amount of money payed to a lender at the maturity date; the sum of all payments
  • MATURITY DATE - When the money borrowed must be paid back by
  • MARKET PRICE - How much the issuer actually has to pay for a bond
  • PRINCIPAL - The amount of money borrowed
  • YIELD - The profit made on a security

TYPES OF BONDS
  • CORPORATE BONDS - Issued by companies & sold to investors; investors give money to a company so they can build / expand & in return investors collect inerest payments by the company; the yield on corporate bonds is higher than government bonds but more risky.
  • MUNICIPAL BONDS - These bonds are issued by states and sold to investors; they have a very low risk
  • GOVERNMENT BONDS - Government bonds are issued by the US Treasury. There are 3 types of government bonds: BILLS, NOTES, and BONDS; bills have a year or less to maturity, notes have 1-10 years to maturity, and bonds have 10+ years to maturity

HOW TO MAKE MONEY OFF BONDS
  • 1. An investor buys bonds with the intention of holding them until they mature so they can profit off the interest
  • 2. Buying and selling bonds at a discount of the face value; the prices of bonds are changed based off predictions of where the interest rates will be
